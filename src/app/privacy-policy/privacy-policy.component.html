Personal Information We Collect

When you visit the Site, we automatically collect certain information about your device, including information about your web browser, IP address, time zone, and some of the cookies that are installed on your device. This information is used to help manage security and protect users of our platform. Additionally, as you browse the Site, we collect information including the specific web pages or products viewed, what websites or search terms referred you to the Site, and information about how you interact with the Site. We refer to this automatically-collected information as "Device Information".

We collect Device Information using the following technologies:

  • • "Cookies" are data files that are placed on your device or computer and often include an anonymous unique identifier. For more information about cookies, and how to disable cookies, visit https://www.allaboutcookies.org.
  • • "Log files" track actions occurring on the Site, and collect data including your IP address, browser type, Internet service provider, referring/exit pages, and date/time stamps.
  • • "Web beacons", "tags", and "pixels" are electronic files used to record information about how you browse the Site.

Additionally, when you make a purchase or attempt to make a purchase through the Site, we collect certain information from you, including your name, billing address, shipping address, payment information (including credit card numbers, account information, etc.), email address, and phone number. We refer to this information as "Order Information". We use this for internal purposes to understand our audience and who is seeking our community. For more information on payment and billing, please see our Terms of Service (section 6).

When we talk about "Personal Information" in this Privacy Policy, we are talking both about Device Information and Order Information, as well as the information included below.

Data Breach Notification Procedure

We take the protection of your personal information seriously. In the unlikely event that a data breach occurs involving your personal data, we will take prompt action to assess, contain, and mitigate the incident, and to notify affected individuals and regulators as required by applicable law.

Definition of a Data Breach

A data breach means any unauthorized access to, disclosure of, loss of, alteration of, or destruction of personal information that could result in harm to individuals, including but not limited to identity theft, fraud, reputational damage, or loss of confidentiality.

Internal Response and Assessment

Upon discovery or suspicion of a breach, we shall:

  • • Immediately secure our systems to prevent further unauthorized access.
  • • Conduct an internal investigation to determine the scope, cause, and impact of the incident.
  • • Document all findings and corrective actions.
  • • Notify relevant management and, where appropriate, our data protection officer or designated privacy representative.

Notification Timeframes

We shall provide notification without undue delay and in accordance with applicable law:

  • • European Union / EEA (GDPR): We shall notify the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of a personal data breach, unless it is unlikely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals. Affected individuals will also be notified without undue delay if the breach is likely to result in a high risk to their rights and freedoms.
  • • United States (including Rhode Island): We shall notify affected individuals as soon as practicable, and no later than 45 days after confirmation of the breach, in compliance with R.I. Gen. Laws § 11-49.3 and applicable state laws.
  • • California (CCPA/CPRA): Where applicable, we shall provide notice consistent with the California Civil Code §§ 1798.29 and 1798.82, including notice to the California Attorney General if more than 500 residents are affected.
  • • Other jurisdictions (including Israel): We shall comply with all mandatory reporting and notification requirements as defined by local data protection laws.

Method of Notification

We shall notify affected individuals using one or more of the following methods:

  • • Email to the address associated with the user's account;
  • • Prominent notice on our website or platform (where direct contact details are unavailable);
  • • Written notice by postal mail if required by law;
  • • Direct communication through account alerts or other electronic means.

Information Included in the Notification

Each breach notification shall include, to the extent known and legally permissible:

  • • A summary of the nature of the breach;
  • • The categories and approximate number of individuals affected;
  • • The types of personal data involved;
  • • The steps we have taken or will take to address and mitigate the breach;
  • • Recommendations for steps affected individuals can take to protect themselves; and
  • • Contact information for our privacy or data protection representative for further inquiries.

Regulatory and Law Enforcement Cooperation

We shall cooperate with relevant data protection authorities, law enforcement, and other regulators as required by law. Where appropriate, we may delay individual notifications if requested by law enforcement to avoid impeding an active investigation.

Recordkeeping

All breaches, whether or not they require notification, shall be recorded and maintained for compliance and audit purposes. These records shall include details of the incident, its effects, and the remedial actions taken.

Children's Privacy

Our services are not directed to individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 18. If we become aware that we have inadvertently collected such information, we shall take steps to delete it.

California-Specific Protections (CPRA)

Under the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), additional protections apply to the personal information of minors:

  • • Children Under 13: We do not knowingly sell or share the personal information of children under 13 years of age.
  • • Minors Ages 13-15: We do not sell or share the personal information of minors ages 13 to 15 without affirmative authorization from the minor.
  • • Minors Ages 16-17: We do not sell or share the personal information of minors ages 16 to 17 without affirmative authorization from the minor or their parent/guardian.

However, as stated above, our platform is designed for users 18 and older, and we rely on users to provide truthful information about their age.

Data Security and Retention

We take commercially reasonable steps to protect your information from loss, misuse, and unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. Please understand, however, that no security system is impenetrable. We cannot guarantee the security of our databases or the databases of the third parties with which we may share such information, nor can we guarantee that the information you supply is not going to be intercepted while being transmitted over the internet. In particular, email sent to us may not be secure, and you should therefore take special care in deciding what information you send to us via email.

We retain your personal information only for as long as is reasonably necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, as outlined in this Privacy Policy, or as required by applicable law. The retention period may vary depending on the category of personal information and the nature of our relationship with you. Below is a summary of our standard retention periods:

• Identifiers (e.g., name, email address, phone number): Retained for up to 7 years after your last interaction with us, or until your request for deletion, whichever comes first.
• Commercial Information (e.g., purchase history, transaction records): Retained for 7 years for accounting, warranty, and business reporting purposes.
• Internet or Electronic Network Activity (e.g., browsing history, IP address, device data): Retained for up to 2 years, unless retained longer for fraud prevention or security investigations.
• Geolocation Data (approximate location): Retained for up to 1 year to optimize user experience and ensure regional compliance.
• Payment Information (e.g., credit card details): Not stored by us directly; securely processed and retained by our payment processor in accordance with PCI DSS standards. Transactional metadata may be retained for 7 years.
• User Account Information (e.g., login credentials, preferences): Retained for the duration of your active account and for up to 2 years following account closure or inactivity.
• Customer Service Records (e.g., support messages, complaints): Retained for 5 years to resolve disputes and ensure service quality.
• Sensitive Personal Information (e.g., financial account credentials, government IDs, health data): Retained only as necessary to complete transactions or fulfill legal or regulatory obligations, and generally for no longer than 1 year, unless otherwise required by law.

After the applicable retention period expires, your data shall be securely deleted, anonymized, or aggregated for statistical purposes. You may request deletion of your personal information at any time, subject to our need to retain certain data to comply with legal obligations or resolve disputes.

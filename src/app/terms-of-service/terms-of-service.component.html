6.1 Payment Processing

All payments on the platform are processed through our third-party payment processor, Stripe, Inc. ("Stripe"). By using the Services, you agree to be bound by Stripe's Services Agreement and applicable terms available at https://stripe.com/legal.

6.2 Platform Service Fee

VouchHer charges a 5% platform service fee on all completed transactions. This fee is calculated on the total transaction amount and is deducted from the payment to the business owner. Example: If a client pays $100 for a service, the business owner receives $95 and VouchHer retains $5 as the platform fee.

6.3 Payment Authorization

When a business owner accepts a service request, the client authorizes VouchHer (via Stripe) to charge the agreed-upon amount to the client's payment method.

6.4 Escrow Process

VouchHer operates an escrow system to protect both clients and business owners:

  • Step 1: Payment Capture - When a service request is accepted, the client's payment method is charged and funds are held in escrow by VouchHer (via Stripe).
  • Step 2: Service Completion - The business owner performs the agreed-upon services according to the contract terms.
  • Step 3: Completion Request - When the business owner completes the work, they submit a "completion request" through the platform.
  • Step 4: Client Confirmation - The client receives a notification and has the opportunity to confirm completion, request changes, or dispute.
  • Step 5: Automatic Release - If the client does not respond within 14 business days of the completion request, funds are automatically released to the business owner.

6.5 Fund Disbursement to Business Owners

Once funds are released from escrow, the business owner's VouchHer balance is credited (total amount minus 5% platform fee). Business owners may withdraw funds to their linked bank account with withdrawals typically processing within 2-5 business days. Minimum withdrawal amount: $10.

6.6 Payment Method Requirements

Clients must provide a valid payment method (credit cards, debit cards, ACH bank transfers). Business owners must provide valid bank account information to receive disbursements.

6.7 Failed Payments

If a payment fails, the client will be notified and given the opportunity to update their payment method. The service request may be cancelled if payment cannot be processed. Business owners will be notified of payment failures.
